The provided sequence XML defines a Rabimodulated pulse sequence with mw_freq as the scanned parameter. The active instructions first polarize the NV, then perform a detection used as the true 0-level reference. The optional 1-level reference block is skipped because full_expt is 0. The sequence then applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 5.2e-08 s, mod_depth = 1, and switch_delay = 1e-07 s, followed by the second detection. Thus readout 1 is the reference readout before the driven pulse and readout 2 is the signal readout after the 52 ns modulated Rabi pulse.

The scan covers 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps with two averages. The raw traces are noisy and have average-to-average baseline offsets, so I compared the signal readout against the reference rather than relying on absolute signal level. The combined signal-reference contrast has repeated frequency-localized deviations: a negative contrast point near 3.855 GHz that appears in both averages, and a stronger high-frequency contrast feature around 3.910-3.915 GHz. These features are not fully smooth, but they are larger than the surrounding point-to-point scatter and are specific to the signal/reference contrast expected from the active Rabimodulated pODMR sequence.

Decision: resonance_present.

The provided sequence XML is the active Rabimodulated sequence, scanning mw_freq from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz. In the active instructions, full_expt = 0, so the optional 1-level reference block is skipped. The executed readouts are therefore: readout 1 is the true 0-level reference acquired immediately after optical polarization and before the microwave pulse, and readout 2 is the signal acquired after the microwave pulse. The pulse applied before the signal readout is rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 5.2e-08 s, i.e. 52 ns, and mod_depth = 1 from the provided XML/variable values.

For this pulse sequence, a pODMR resonance should appear as a localized, reproducible microwave-frequency-dependent contrast in the post-pulse signal relative to the pre-pulse reference, typically a fluorescence reduction near resonance after population transfer out of the bright state. The raw readouts do not show a clear localized dip in the post-pulse signal. Instead, the traces fluctuate by a few counts with broad high-frequency drift and only two averages; the per-average overlays are not mutually consistent enough to support a distinct resonance feature. The largest apparent changes are broad and sign-inconsistent with the expected post-pulse darkening.

Decision: resonance absent.

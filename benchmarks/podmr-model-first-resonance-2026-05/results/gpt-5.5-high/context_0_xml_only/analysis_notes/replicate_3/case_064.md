The provided sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The active executed sequence polarizes the NV, records a first detection as the polarized/no-microwave reference, waits, applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1, then records the second detection as the microwave-affected readout. Because full_expt = 0, the optional 1-level reference block is skipped even though it is present in the XML.

The combined raw readouts are noisy and the two averages have a large offset from each other. Comparing the MW-affected readout to the preceding reference gives negative contrast at a few frequencies, most notably near 3.865 GHz and 3.910 GHz. The 3.865 GHz contrast minimum is strongly helped by a high reference point, while the 3.910 GHz point is a sharper low in the MW readout. These features are isolated rather than forming a stable dip across neighboring frequency points, and the per-average traces do not show a clean, reproducible resonance line shape.

Given the active sequence and readout roles, I do not see enough coherent frequency-dependent contrast to call a pODMR resonance. The data are consistent with noise and drift in a two-average scan rather than a resolved resonance.

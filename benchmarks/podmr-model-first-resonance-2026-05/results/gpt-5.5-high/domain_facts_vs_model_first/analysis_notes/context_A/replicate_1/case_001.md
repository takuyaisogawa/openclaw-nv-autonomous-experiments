Active sequence: Rabimodulated.xml, with mw_freq scanned from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz. The active readouts are the initial detection after optical polarization, which serves as the m_S = 0 / bright reference, and the later detection after rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time, which is the microwave-pulse signal readout. The optional m_S = +1 reference block is gated by full_expt, and full_expt = 0, so that block is inactive.

Using the provided sequence XML, mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With the stated setup scale of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, this pulse is close to a pi pulse. Therefore an on-resonance pODMR response should produce a large reduction in the post-pulse signal readout relative to the bright reference, with a scale approaching the stated 22% contrast if the drive is effective.

The combined raw traces do not show such a response. The post-pulse readout is often comparable to or higher than the reference and has isolated fluctuations, including high points near the upper end of the scan, rather than a coherent dip or resonance feature. The two stored averages differ substantially and appear consistent with drift/tracking cadence effects, so they do not provide a strong repeatability check. Given the expected pulse strength, the absence of a clear signal suppression means the evidence is insufficient for a pODMR resonance.

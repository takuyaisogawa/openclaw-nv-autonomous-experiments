The provided XML and raw export identify the active sequence as Rabimodulated.xml while sweeping mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The instruction block first polarizes and detects a true m_s = 0 reference, then skips the optional m_s = 1 reference because full_expt = 0, then applies one modulated Rabi pulse and detects the pulsed readout. Thus readout 1 is the m_s = 0 reference and readout 2 is the readout after the microwave pulse.

The active pulse has length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1. With the supplied setup fact that the Rabi frequency is about 10 MHz at mod_depth = 1, this is close to a pi pulse on resonance, so a real pODMR resonance should produce a substantial fluorescence drop in the pulsed readout relative to the m_s = 0 reference, on the order of the 22% contrast scale if the pulse is well matched.

The combined traces do not show that behavior. Readout 2 is often similar to or higher than readout 1, and the reference-normalized pulsed readout only has small irregular dips around a few scan points, roughly 5% below the reference at the deepest points, with adjacent points recovering or moving oppositely. Both readouts also show baseline motion across the scan, and the two stored averages mainly reflect tracking cadence rather than a strong independent repeatability check. The per-average traces therefore do not establish a stable resonance feature.

Given the near-pi-pulse conditions, the expected resonant contrast should be clearer than the small nonmonotonic deviations seen here. I classify this case as resonance absent.

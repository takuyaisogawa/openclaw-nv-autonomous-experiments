The provided sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz. The active instructions acquire a true mS=0 reference first by optical polarization followed by detection, then skip the optional mS=1 reference because full_expt = 0, then apply rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time and detect again. Thus readout 1 is the polarized reference and readout 2 is the signal after the microwave pulse.

The relevant pulse is length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns with mod_depth = 1 in the provided sequence/variable values. With the stated setup Rabi scale of about 10 MHz at mod_depth = 1, 52 ns is close to a pi pulse, so on resonance the second readout should darken by roughly the setup contrast between mS=0 and mS=+1, about 22%.

The combined data show readout 1 staying near 39 to 41 counts through the center of the scan, while readout 2 drops sharply to about 30.3 to 30.6 at 3.875 to 3.880 GHz and then recovers. Relative to the local off-resonance level near 38, that is about a 20% drop, close to the expected contrast scale. The per-average traces are only two averages and can reflect tracking cadence, but both averages contribute to the central depression rather than producing a single isolated point. Because the dip is localized in frequency, has the expected sign for the post-pulse readout, and has the expected magnitude for a near-pi pulse, this is a pODMR resonance.

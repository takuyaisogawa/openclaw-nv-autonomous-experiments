The executed sequence first polarizes and detects the bright m_S = 0 reference, waits, skips the m_S = 1 reference branch because full_expt = 0, then applies a rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse and detects again. Thus readout 1 is the local m_S = 0/reference readout and readout 2 is the post-microwave-pulse signal readout.

The provided sequence XML has length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1. With the stated setup calibration, mod_depth = 1 gives roughly 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so a 52 ns pulse is close to a pi pulse on resonance. If a pODMR resonance were present in this scan, the post-pulse signal readout should show a clear fluorescence loss relative to the m_S = 0 reference, on the order of the setup contrast scale rather than a small count-level fluctuation.

The combined readouts do not show that behavior. Readout 2 is usually comparable to readout 1, sometimes above it and sometimes below it. The largest combined deficit is only about 3.27 counts at 3.890 GHz, roughly 6.9 percent of the local reference, and it is not a robust resonance-shaped feature across adjacent points. The per-average overlay shows large opposite background/tracking drift between the two stored averages, so the individual averages do not provide strong repeatability evidence for a resonance. Given the near-pi pulse settings and expected 22 percent contrast scale, the observed data are more consistent with no detectable pODMR resonance in this scan.

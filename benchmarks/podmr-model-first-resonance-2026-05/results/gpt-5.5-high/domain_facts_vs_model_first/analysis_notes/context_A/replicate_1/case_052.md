Active sequence: Rabimodulated.xml, varying mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The sequence first polarizes and detects a bright m_S=0 reference, then because full_expt = 0 it skips the separate m_S=+1 reference, applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time, and performs the second detection. Thus readout 1 is the bright reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave signal readout.

The provided sequence settings give mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With the stated setup calibration, this is approximately a pi pulse on resonance, so a strong resonance could approach the 22 percent m_S=0 to m_S=+1 contrast scale. The observed combined signal/reference contrast is much smaller, peaking at about 6 percent, but it is localized: readout 2 is suppressed relative to readout 1 around 3.845-3.855 GHz, with the largest points near 3.845 and 3.850 GHz. Outside that region the readout difference changes sign and is mostly near zero.

The stored per-average traces show tracking drift and should not be treated as a strong independent repeatability test, but both averages still contribute some positive signal suppression in the same 3.845-3.855 GHz region. Because the feature is frequency-localized and appears in the correct readout role for the active pi-pulse pODMR sequence, I classify this as a weak resonance present rather than absent.

The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The sequence first polarizes and detects a true mS = 0 reference, then skips the mS = 1 reference because full_expt = 0, then applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1 before the second detection. Thus readout 1 is the zero-state reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave signal readout.

At mod_depth = 1 the setup Rabi frequency is about 10 MHz, so a 52 ns pulse is close to a pi pulse on resonance. A real resonance should therefore appear mainly as reduced PL in readout 2 relative to readout 1 at the resonant microwave frequency, with the full available contrast scale about 22%.

The combined data show a localized negative contrast around 3.895 GHz: readout 1 is about 50.0 while readout 2 is about 45.38, a drop of about 4.62 counts or 9.2% relative to the reference. The two stored averages both show the same direction at this point, though the averages are limited and may partly reflect tracking cadence. Neighboring points recover substantially, so this is more consistent with a pODMR dip than with a broad drift-only effect. The dip is weaker than the nominal full contrast but is large enough and localized enough to count as a resonance in this noisy scan.

Active sequence: Rabimodulated.xml, sweeping mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The operative sequence has full_expt = 0, so the intermediate m_S = +1 reference branch is skipped. The two acquired readouts are therefore the initial post-polarization bright m_S = 0 reference, followed by the post-microwave Rabi-pulse detection.

The provided sequence XML and saved variable values indicate mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With the stated setup calibration of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, a 52 ns pulse is close to a pi pulse. If the sweep crossed a real pODMR resonance, the post-pulse readout should show a comparatively strong fluorescence decrease relative to the bright reference, with a scale approaching the available 22% contrast in favorable conditions.

The combined data do not show that behavior. The largest normalized post-pulse drop relative to the reference is only about 5.7% near 3.900 GHz, while other points show comparable excursions of the opposite sign. Both raw readouts also share broad drift across the scan, and the two stored averages mainly reflect tracking changes rather than a stable independent replication of a resonance. The evidence is therefore consistent with no clear pODMR resonance in this scan.

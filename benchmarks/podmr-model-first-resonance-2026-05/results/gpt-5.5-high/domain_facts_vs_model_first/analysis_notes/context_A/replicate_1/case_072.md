The active sequence is Rabimodulated with mw_freq swept from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz. With full_expt = 0, the optional m_S = +1 reference block is skipped. The two active detections are therefore: readout 1 as the polarized m_S = 0 / bright reference, followed by readout 2 after the modulated Rabi pulse.

The provided XML sets mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. Given the stated roughly 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, this is approximately a pi pulse on resonance, so a real pODMR resonance should appear as reduced readout 2 relative to readout 1, with an ideal contrast scale around 22%.

The combined data show readout 2 locally suppressed relative to readout 1 around 3.885-3.890 GHz: approximately 43.9 versus 47.2 and 43.7 versus 47.0, or about 7% contrast. This feature is present in both stored averages, though the stored averages should be treated cautiously because they can reflect tracking cadence. There are other noisy low points, and the observed contrast is well below the expected full pi-pulse contrast, so this is not a strong clean resonance. Still, the coherent two-point dip near the expected driven-readout channel is enough to classify the case as resonance present with low confidence.

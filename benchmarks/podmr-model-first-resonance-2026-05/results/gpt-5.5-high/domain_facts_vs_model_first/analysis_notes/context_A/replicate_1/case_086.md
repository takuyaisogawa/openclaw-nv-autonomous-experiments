The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The instruction block first polarizes and detects the true bright m_S = 0 level, then because full_expt = 0 it skips the separate m_S = +1 reference block and applies one rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse before the second detection. Thus readout 1 is the bright reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave readout.

The provided XML and exported variable values indicate mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With the setup fact of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, this pulse is near a pi pulse, so a real resonance should produce a substantial reduction in the post-microwave readout relative to the bright reference, on the order of the stated 22% contrast scale for a well-driven transition.

The combined data do not show that. Readout 2 is only about 1.3% lower than readout 1 on average, and the largest local reductions are around 5%, comparable to point-to-point scatter and not a stable resonance-scale dip. Several frequencies have readout 2 higher than readout 1. The two stored averages show drifting/scattered traces rather than a reproducible frequency-localized feature, and the note about stored averages cautions that they mostly reflect tracking cadence. I therefore judge the pODMR resonance to be absent in this scan.

Active sequence: Rabimodulated.xml, varying mw_freq from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The provided sequence has full_expt = 0, so the conditional +1 reference acquisition is inactive. The actual executed readouts are therefore the bright m_S = 0 reference after optical polarization, then the post-microwave signal readout after one rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time call.

Pulse settings from the provided sequence are mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With the stated setup calibration, mod_depth = 1 gives about 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so 52 ns is close to a pi pulse. If the swept microwave frequency crossed a real pODMR resonance, the signal readout should show a sizeable, localized reduction relative to the m_S = 0 reference, on the order of the setup contrast scale rather than a small common-mode drift.

The two combined raw readouts mostly track the same slow downward drift across the scan. The post-microwave readout relative to the reference is small and irregular: signal/reference ranges roughly from 0.955 to 1.037 and the sign of the difference alternates between neighboring points. The largest apparent dips are isolated points near 3.855, 3.875, 3.905, and 3.915 GHz rather than a coherent resonance feature. The per-average overlays show the stored averages separated by tracking level, so they do not provide strong independent confirmation of a reproducible dip.

Decision: resonance absent. The observed variations are much smaller and less structured than expected for a near-pi pODMR pulse at mod_depth = 1, and are better explained by drift/noise plus tracking cadence than by a real resonance.

The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The XML executes an initial polarization and detection, then skips the optional 1-level reference branch because full_expt = 0, then applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time followed by the second detection. Thus readout 1 is the polarized m_S = 0 reference, and readout 2 is the post-microwave-pulse signal readout.

The relevant pulse settings are length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1. With the stated setup estimate of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, this is approximately a pi pulse, so a true resonance should produce a fairly clear reduction of the post-pulse signal relative to the m_S = 0 reference, with possible contrast on the order of the stated 22% scale.

The combined readouts do not show a coherent resonance-shaped signal. The normalized signal/reference contrast fluctuates around zero, with isolated point excursions such as near 3.855 GHz but no stable adjacent-point dip or clear line shape. The per-average traces mainly show opposing slow trends consistent with tracking cadence, so I do not treat those stored averages as independent repeatability evidence. Given the near-pi pulse conditions, the absence of a clear contrast feature supports resonance_absent.

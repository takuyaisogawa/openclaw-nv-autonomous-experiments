The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The sequence first polarizes and detects a true m_S = 0 reference, then applies one rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse and detects again. Because full_expt = 0, the optional m_S = +1 reference block is inactive, so the two combined readouts should be interpreted as reference readout and post-pulse readout rather than two independent state references.

The active pulse settings are mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With the stated setup calibration, mod_depth = 1 gives an approximately 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so 52 ns is close to a pi-pulse condition on resonance. If a pODMR resonance were present and well addressed, the post-pulse readout should show a clear fluorescence depression relative to the polarized reference, on the order of the setup contrast scale of about 22% between m_S = 0 and m_S = +1.

The combined post-pulse minus reference readout varies irregularly across the sweep. The largest negative normalized excursion is about -6.9% near 3.890 GHz, with other negative excursions of comparable scale near 3.830 GHz and 3.850 GHz and positive excursions elsewhere. This is much smaller than the expected near-pi-pulse contrast and does not form a distinct, isolated resonance-like dip. The per-average traces also show broad tracking drift and opposite baseline movement between averages, consistent with the note that stored averages often reflect tracking cadence rather than a strong repeatability test.

Decision: resonance_absent. The observed fluctuations are too small and too non-specific relative to the expected contrast for a 52 ns, mod_depth = 1 pulse to support a pODMR resonance call.

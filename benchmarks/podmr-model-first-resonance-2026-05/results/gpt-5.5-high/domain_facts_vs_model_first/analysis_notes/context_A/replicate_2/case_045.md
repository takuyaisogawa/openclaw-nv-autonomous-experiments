Active sequence: Rabimodulated.xml, varying mw_freq from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The sequence first polarizes and detects a true mS=0 bright reference, then because full_expt is 0 it skips the separate mS=1 reference block, applies one rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse, and detects again. Thus readout 1 is the bright reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave signal readout.

The provided XML variables set mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With the stated setup Rabi frequency of about 10 MHz at mod_depth 1, this is approximately a pi-pulse duration, so an on-resonance point should produce a strong population transfer and a fluorescence drop on the order of the setup contrast scale, about 22% for a good mS=0 to mS=+1 contrast.

The combined readouts do not show that behavior. The mean readout 1 and readout 2 levels are essentially equal, and the largest isolated negative excursion of readout 2 relative to readout 1 is only about 7% at one scan point, with neighboring points and stored averages not forming a coherent resonance dip. The per-average traces mainly show offset/tracking changes rather than repeatable resonance structure. I therefore classify this pODMR scan as resonance absent.

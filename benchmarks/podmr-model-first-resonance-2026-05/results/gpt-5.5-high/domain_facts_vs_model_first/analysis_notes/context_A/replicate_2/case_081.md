I used inputs/sequence.xml to identify the active sequence as Rabimodulated.xml. The scan varies mw_freq, and the executed body first polarizes and detects a true m_S = 0 reference readout, then, because full_expt = 0, skips the separate m_S = +1 reference block and performs one rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse before the second detection. Thus readout 1 is the polarized reference and readout 2 is the post-Rabi-pulse signal readout.

The relevant pulse settings from the provided XML are mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 5.2e-08 s, rounded at a 250 MHz sample rate. With the supplied setup facts, a 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1 makes 52 ns close to a pi pulse, so a true pODMR resonance should produce a clear suppression of readout 2 relative to the m_S = 0 reference on resonance, with a possible contrast scale much larger than the small count-to-count scatter in these traces.

The plotted and exported raw readouts do not show such a clear resonance. Readout 2 fluctuates above and below readout 1 across the frequency scan, with a few isolated negative deviations but no coherent dip with the expected magnitude or stable line shape. The per-average overlay also looks dominated by tracking/noise cadence rather than independent repeatable resonance structure. Given the near-pi pulse condition, the absence of a strong readout-2 contrast feature argues that no pODMR resonance is present in this measurement.

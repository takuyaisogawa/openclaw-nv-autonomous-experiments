Active sequence: Rabimodulated.xml. The sequence first polarizes and detects a true bright m_S = 0 reference, then skips the explicit m_S = +1 reference because full_expt = 0, then applies a modulated Rabi pulse and detects the signal readout. Thus readout 1 is the bright reference and readout 2 is the microwave-response readout.

The provided sequence variables give mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With the stated setup calibration of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, this is close to a pi pulse, so an on-resonance transition should make the second readout drop relative to the first by a sizable fraction of the 22% contrast scale. The largest ratio dip is near 3.905 GHz, where readout 2 is about 24.1 while readout 1 is about 27.6, roughly a 13% reduction. The neighboring point near 3.900 GHz is also lower, and the feature appears in both stored averages, although the stored averages are not a strong independent repeatability test because they can reflect tracking cadence.

There are noisy point-to-point variations and the observed contrast is not the full expected 22%, but the localized two-point dip in the post-pulse readout, not mirrored as a comparable drop in the bright reference, is consistent with a pODMR resonance.

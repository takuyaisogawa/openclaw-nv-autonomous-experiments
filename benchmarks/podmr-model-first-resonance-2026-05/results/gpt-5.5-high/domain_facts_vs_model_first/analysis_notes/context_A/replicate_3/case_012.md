The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml, varying mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. With full_expt = 0, the only active measurements are the true bright reference after optical polarization and then the post-Rabi-pulse signal readout; the optional m_S = +1 reference block is skipped. Thus readout 1 is the m_S = 0 reference and readout 2 is the signal after the microwave pulse.

The provided sequence XML gives mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With the stated setup calibration, mod_depth = 1 corresponds to about 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so 52 ns is approximately a pi pulse on resonance. A real pulsed ODMR resonance should therefore produce a clear reduction of readout 2 relative to readout 1, potentially on the order of the setup contrast scale rather than a tiny fluctuation.

The combined data do not show a stable resonance-like dip. The largest apparent normalized reductions of readout 2 relative to readout 1 are about 8% near 3.860 GHz and 7.5% near 3.885 GHz, much smaller than the nominal 22% contrast scale for an on-resonance pi pulse. Neighboring points alternate sign, and readout 2 is often brighter than the reference. The per-average traces are also dominated by drift/scatter rather than a consistent lineshape, and the stored averages are not a strong independent repeatability test. I therefore classify this scan as resonance absent.

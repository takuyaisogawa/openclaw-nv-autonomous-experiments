The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The sequence first polarizes and detects a true m_S = 0 reference, then, because full_expt = 0, skips the separate m_S = +1 reference block and applies a single rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time before the second detection. Thus readout 1 is the reference/normalization readout and readout 2 is the microwave-pulse signal readout.

The active pulse settings are length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1. With the stated setup scale of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, a 52 ns pulse is near a pi pulse, so a real resonance should be capable of producing close to the full available m_S = 0 to m_S = +1 contrast. The expected maximum contrast scale is about 22%.

The data show a pronounced feature only in the signal readout: readout 2 falls from a typical off-feature level around 47-49 counts to about 39 counts at 3.880 GHz, with neighboring points also depressed around 3.875-3.885 GHz. Readout 1 remains near 47-50 counts over the same region and does not show a matching drop. The depth of the signal dip is roughly 18-20% of the off-feature signal level, consistent with the stated contrast scale for a near-pi pulse. The two stored averages both show the same broad signal depression around the same frequency region, although their absolute levels differ, so this is not just a single averaged point artifact. Stored averages are not treated as a strong independent repeatability test, but they do support the same qualitative feature.

Decision: a pODMR resonance is present.

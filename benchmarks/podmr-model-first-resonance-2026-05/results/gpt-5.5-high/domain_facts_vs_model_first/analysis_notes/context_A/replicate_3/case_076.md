The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml, scanning mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The sequence first polarizes and detects a true m_S = 0 reference, then the full_expt-controlled m_S = 1 reference block is skipped because full_expt = 0, then it applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1 before the final detection. Thus readout 1 is the m_S = 0 reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave signal readout.

Using the provided setup facts, mod_depth = 1 gives about a 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so a 52 ns pulse is close to a pi pulse. On a real resonance this should drive substantial population transfer and create a signal readout dip approaching the setup contrast scale, about 22 percent, with a frequency response broad enough to affect multiple 5 MHz scan points.

The combined traces do not show that behavior. Readout 2 is similar to readout 1 across the scan, with mean values essentially equal. The strongest normalized signal drop is at 3.920 GHz, where readout 2 is about 6.3 percent below readout 1, but it is narrow and not accompanied by the expected broad, high-contrast pi-pulse response. The two stored averages are not strong independent repeatability evidence because they may reflect tracking cadence. I therefore treat the 3.920 GHz point as a fluctuation or drift-sensitive feature rather than a clear pODMR resonance.

The provided sequence XML is Rabimodulated.xml. With full_expt = 0, the optional m_S = +1 reference block is inactive. The active sequence first polarizes and detects the bright m_S = 0 reference, then applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1, then detects the post-pulse signal. Thus readout 1 is the 0-level reference and readout 2 is the microwave-pulse readout.

For this setup, mod_depth = 1 corresponds to about a 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so a 52 ns pulse is close to a pi pulse. If the scanned microwave frequency hit a pODMR resonance, the post-pulse readout should show a sizeable local reduction relative to the 0 reference, on the order of the stated 22% contrast scale for an effective pi pulse.

The combined readouts instead show readout 2/readout 1 varying only roughly from 0.95 to 1.04, with the largest deficits about 4-5% and no clean, isolated resonance-like dip. The two stored averages mainly differ by tracking level, and their overlays do not provide a strong independent repeatability test. The observed variation is small compared with the expected pi-pulse contrast and is not convincing evidence for a pODMR resonance.

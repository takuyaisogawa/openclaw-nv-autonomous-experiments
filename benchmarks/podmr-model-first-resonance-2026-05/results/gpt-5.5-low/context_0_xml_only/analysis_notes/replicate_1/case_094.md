Sequence XML and raw export identify the active sequence as Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The active microwave operation is rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 5.2e-08 s (52 ns) and mod_depth = 1. Because full_expt = 0, the optional one-level reference block is inactive. The two active detections are therefore the initial true-0/reference readout after polarization and the final readout after the modulated Rabi pulse.

I interpreted readout 1 as the initial reference and readout 2 as the post-pulse signal. The raw traces are noisy and have only two averages, but the signal/reference contrast has its strongest negative excursion near 3.895 GHz, where readout 2 is about 1.81 counts below readout 1 and the ratio is the minimum of the sweep. This low-contrast region is partly continued at nearby high-frequency points rather than being only a completely isolated plotting artifact. Both per-average traces also support reduced post-pulse readout around that point.

Decision: a shallow pODMR resonance is present, with low-to-moderate visual clarity because the feature is noisy and the scan has limited averaging.

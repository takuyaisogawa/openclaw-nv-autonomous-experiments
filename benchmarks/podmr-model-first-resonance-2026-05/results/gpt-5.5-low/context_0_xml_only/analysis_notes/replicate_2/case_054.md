Sequence XML and raw export indicate the active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The sequence first performs polarization and detection for the true 0-level reference readout, then skips the optional 1-level reference block because full_expt = 0, then applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1 before the final detection readout. Thus readout 1 is the reference before the microwave pulse and readout 2 is the microwave-modulated signal readout.

The combined readouts show one pronounced low signal/reference ratio near 3.885 GHz, where readout 2 is much lower than readout 1. However, the trace is based on only two averages and the per-average overlays show large scatter, with readout 2 fluctuating high and low at neighboring frequencies and no stable, coherent dip shape across the sweep. The apparent feature is therefore not convincing enough to call a pODMR resonance in this isolated case.

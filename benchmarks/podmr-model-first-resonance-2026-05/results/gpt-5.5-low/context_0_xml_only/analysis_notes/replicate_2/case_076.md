Sequence inspection:

The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq as the scanned variable. The sweep spans 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The active microwave operation before the signal detection is rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1, after rounding to the 250 MHz sample rate. The XML includes a 1-level reference block, but full_expt = 0, so that block is inactive. Therefore the recorded readouts correspond to the initial true 0-level reference detection after optical polarization and the post-rabi-pulse detection.

Readout assessment:

The two combined readout traces are noisy at the level of about 1 count. The post-pulse readout does not show a clear, repeatable ODMR-like dip or peak across the frequency sweep. There is a pronounced low point in readout 2 near 3.920 GHz, but it is isolated, near the high-frequency edge, and not supported by a consistent resonance-shaped feature in the reference/readout relationship or in the per-average overlay. Other excursions of similar scale appear elsewhere in the scan, and the normalized/differential behavior is not stable enough to identify a resonance.

Decision:

No convincing pODMR resonance is present in this dataset.

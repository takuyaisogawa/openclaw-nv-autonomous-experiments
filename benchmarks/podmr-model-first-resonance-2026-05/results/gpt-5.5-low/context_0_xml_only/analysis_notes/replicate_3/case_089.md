The sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The active instructions first polarize and detect a true 0-level reference, then wait. The nominal 1-level reference block is inactive because full_expt is 0, even though do_adiabatic_inversion is set true; therefore the active measured roles are the initial 0/reference readout and the later signal readout after a modulated Rabi pulse. The active microwave pulse is rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1, followed by detection. The sample rate rounds 52 ns to exactly 13 samples at 250 MHz.

The two combined readouts both vary only by a few counts and share a broad upward drift toward higher microwave frequency. There is no localized dip or peak that is consistent across the two readout channels and the two averages. Features near individual scan points are comparable to the point-to-point noise and are not stable enough to identify as a pODMR resonance. Because the measurement is effectively a reference plus signal readout with only two averages and the visible structure is dominated by common-mode drift, I classify this case as resonance absent.

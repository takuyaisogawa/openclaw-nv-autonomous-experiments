Sequence XML identifies the active sequence as Rabimodulated.xml with a microwave-frequency sweep. The executed instructions first polarize and detect the bright m_S = 0 reference, wait, then because full_expt = 0 skip the separate m_S = +1 reference block, apply rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1, and detect again. Thus readout 1 is the direct post-polarization/0-level reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave-pulse signal readout.

Using the stated setup scale, mod_depth = 1 gives about 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so a 52 ns pulse is close to a pi pulse on resonance. With an m_S = 0 to m_S = +1 contrast scale of about 22%, a genuine pODMR resonance under this sequence should produce a clear localized drop of the post-pulse signal relative to the reference. The raw traces instead mostly track one another with fluctuations of only a few counts around roughly 53, and the most notable dip in readout 2 is not a robust, high-contrast resonance feature relative to the reference or to the two stored averages. The stored averages also should not be overinterpreted as repeatability because they can reflect tracking cadence.

Decision: resonance_absent. The sequence is capable of producing a strong contrast response here, but the observed frequency-dependent contrast is small and not convincing as a pODMR resonance.

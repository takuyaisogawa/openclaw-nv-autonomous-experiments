Active sequence: Rabimodulated.xml, scanning mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The XML sets length_rabi_pulse to 52 ns and mod_depth to 1. full_expt is 0, so the optional mS=+1 reference acquisition block is skipped. The readout roles are therefore readout 1 as the true mS=0 reference after optical polarization, and readout 2 as the signal after the modulated microwave Rabi pulse followed by detection.

Domain expectation: with about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth 1, a 52 ns pulse is close to a pi pulse, so on resonance it should produce substantial population transfer and a sizeable PL decrease in the post-pulse readout relative to the mS=0 reference. Given the stated setup contrast scale of about 22%, a convincing resonance should be much larger and more frequency-localized than ordinary point-to-point scatter.

Observed behavior: the combined post-pulse readout differs from the reference by an average of only about -0.65 counts, roughly -1.3% of the reference level. The largest negative deviations are about -5.2%, and comparable positive deviations also occur. The sign and amplitude fluctuate across the scan rather than forming a clear resonance-shaped dip at a particular microwave frequency. The two stored averages show substantial variability, and those averages should mainly be treated as tracking-cadence samples rather than a strong repeatability test.

Decision: resonance_absent. The sequence is sensitive enough that a true pODMR resonance should be much more pronounced for this pulse duration and modulation depth, while the measured readout differences are small, inconsistent, and not robustly localized.

Active sequence: Rabimodulated.xml, varying mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The instruction flow first performs polarization and detection for a true m_S = 0 reference readout, then skips the optional m_S = +1 reference because full_expt = 0, then applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1 before the final detection. Thus readout 1 is the m_S = 0 reference and readout 2 is the post-Rabi-pulse signal readout.

With the supplied domain calibration, mod_depth = 1 gives about 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so a 52 ns pulse is near a pi pulse and should yield a large pODMR response near resonance, on the order of the setup contrast scale if well driven. The raw traces instead show a shared upward drift across the scan. Comparing readout 2 to readout 1 gives small, sign-changing differences, with no stable localized dip or peak: examples include about -4.9% at 3.830 GHz, +2.1% at 3.835 GHz, -2.6% at 3.875 GHz, +4.8% at 3.885 GHz, -4.2% at 3.900 GHz, and +4.2% at 3.925 GHz. The per-average overlays also do not provide strong independent repeatability, and stored averages may mostly reflect tracking cadence.

Decision: resonance absent. The expected near-pi-pulse contrast is not present; the observed structure is small compared with the approximately 22% contrast scale and is not coherent enough to identify a pODMR resonance.

The provided sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz. The active instructions first acquire a true m_S = 0 reference by polarizing and detecting, then because full_expt = 0 they skip the separate m_S = +1 reference block, then apply one rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse and detect again. Thus readout 1 is the bright 0 reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave signal readout.

The sequence variables show mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With the stated setup scale, a 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth 1 gives about 0.52 Rabi cycles during the pulse, close to a strong inversion condition when on resonance. A real resonance should therefore appear mainly as a dip in the post-pulse readout relative to the bright reference, with a possible scale near the stated 22 percent contrast.

The combined data show readout 1 staying around 38-42 counts without a matching sharp resonance-shaped dip, while readout 2 has a pronounced local minimum near 3.875-3.88 GHz, falling from a roughly 39 count off-resonant level to about 30.8 counts. That is approximately a 21 percent reduction, consistent with the expected m_S = 0 to m_S = +1 contrast for this setup. The same dip appears in both stored averages, although the averages are not treated as a strong independent repeatability test. Given the pulse parameters and readout roles, this is best interpreted as a pODMR resonance.

The provided sequence is Rabimodulated.xml, varying mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz. It first polarizes and detects a true m_S=0 reference, then because full_expt is 0 it skips the separate m_S=+1 reference block and applies the active rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time before the second detection. Thus readout 1 is the bright reference and readout 2 is the microwave-pulse readout.

The sequence variables give length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1. With the stated setup calibration of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, the Rabi period is about 100 ns, so 52 ns is approximately a pi pulse. On resonance this should transfer population toward m_S=+1 and reduce the second readout by up to the setup contrast scale, while the first reference should remain comparatively flat.

The data match that expectation: readout 1 fluctuates around roughly 46 to 49 without a comparable resonant feature, while readout 2 has a localized, multi-point dip near 3.875 to 3.880 GHz down to about 39 counts from a baseline near 46 to 48. The dip depth is around 15 to 18 percent of the local bright level, consistent with a substantial fraction of the stated 22 percent contrast. Although only two stored averages are present and these can reflect tracking cadence, both averages show the same central depression in the pulse readout. This is consistent with a pODMR resonance being present.

The sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq scanned from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The active instructions first acquire a polarized fluorescence reference after adj_polarize and detection; because full_expt is 0, the optional separate m_S=1 reference block is skipped. The second acquired readout is therefore the detection after a rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse.

The relevant pulse is length_rabi_pulse = 5.2e-08 s, rounded at 250 MHz sample rate to 52 ns, with mod_depth = 1. Given the stated setup calibration of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth 1, this pulse is approximately a pi pulse. On resonance it should transfer population from m_S=0 toward m_S=+1 and reduce fluorescence by a sizable fraction of the stated 22% contrast.

The raw data match this expectation. Readout 1 stays near 46 to 49 counts and functions as the m_S=0 reference. Readout 2 is near the same level off resonance but shows a deep, localized dip at scan points around 3.870 to 3.880 GHz, reaching about 39.6 counts. Relative to an off-resonance level around 47 counts, that is roughly a 16% fluorescence reduction, well within the expected scale for a near-pi pulse with imperfect contrast and readout noise.

The per-average overlay shows the same feature in both stored averages, though stored averages should be interpreted cautiously because they may reflect tracking cadence rather than independent repeatability. The line shape is still localized, has the correct sign for an ODMR transition after the active pulse, and is much larger than the point-to-point variation in the reference channel. I therefore classify this case as resonance_present.

Active sequence: Rabimodulated.xml, varying mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The active branch has full_expt = 0, so the optional m_S = +1 reference is skipped. Readout 1 is the detection immediately after optical polarization, serving as the bright m_S = 0 reference. Readout 2 is the detection after a Rabi-modulated microwave pulse, followed by detection.

The sequence XML sets length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1. With the stated setup scale of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, this pulse is approximately a pi pulse on resonance. Given the reported 22% contrast scale between m_S = 0 and m_S = +1, an on-resonance response should appear as a clear localized decrease of the post-pulse readout relative to the polarized readout, not just a small crossing or broad drift.

The combined raw traces show readout 2 starting several counts below readout 1 at the low-frequency end, then rising to meet or exceed readout 1 toward the high-frequency side. That behavior is not a localized pODMR dip centered at a resonance; it is dominated by broad drift/cadence structure. The two stored averages also have strong opposing trends, consistent with tracking cadence effects rather than independent repeatability of a spectral feature. Because the expected near-pi-pulse contrast is large enough that a true resonance should be more distinct than this, I do not identify a reliable pODMR resonance in this scan.

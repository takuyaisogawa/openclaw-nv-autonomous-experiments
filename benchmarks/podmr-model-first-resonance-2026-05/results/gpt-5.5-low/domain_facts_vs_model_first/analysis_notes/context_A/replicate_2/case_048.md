The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The sequence first polarizes and detects the true m_S = 0 reference, then because full_expt = 0 it skips the optional m_S = +1 reference branch, then applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time followed by the signal detection. Thus readout 1 is the 0-level reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave signal readout.

The saved variable values give length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1. With the provided setup calibration, the Rabi frequency is about 10 MHz at mod_depth = 1, so 52 ns is approximately a pi pulse. If a pODMR resonance were present, the second readout should show a clear resonance-associated fluorescence reduction relative to the first readout, on the order of the setup contrast scale between m_S = 0 and m_S = +1 rather than only small count-level wandering.

The combined raw readouts do not show a robust, localized ODMR dip in readout 2 relative to readout 1. The readout difference fluctuates across the sweep, changes sign at several points, and the two stored averages are not a strong repeatability check because they may mostly reflect tracking cadence. The largest apparent deviations are comparable to the baseline scatter and do not form a convincing resonance feature near the swept frequencies. I therefore classify this case as resonance absent.

The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml, varying mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz. In the provided sequence XML, full_expt is 0, so the "Acquire 1 level reference" block is inactive. The active readouts are therefore readout 1 as the polarized true m_S=0 reference and readout 2 as the detection after a single rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse.

The pulse parameters are mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. Using the stated setup scale, mod_depth = 1 gives about a 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so a 52 ns pulse is close to a half-cycle/pi pulse on resonance. If the scanned microwave frequency crossed a real pODMR resonance, readout 2 should show a clear, localized fluorescence drop relative to the m_S=0 reference on the order of the available contrast scale, about 22% for full m_S=0 to m_S=+1 contrast.

The raw traces do not show that behavior. Readout 2 is usually close to readout 1 and is often higher, without a consistent localized dip at a frequency point. The two stored averages have visibly different baselines and fluctuations, which is consistent with tracking cadence and shot/noise variation rather than a repeatable resonance signature. Given the near-pi pulse condition, the absence of a large, frequency-localized reduction in the post-pulse readout argues against a present pODMR resonance.

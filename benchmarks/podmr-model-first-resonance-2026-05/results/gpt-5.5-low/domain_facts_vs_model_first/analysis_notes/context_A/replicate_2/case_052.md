The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml varying mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz. The sequence first polarizes and detects a true m_S=0 reference readout, then because full_expt=0 it skips the optional m_S=1 reference block, then applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1 before the final detection. Thus readout 1 is the bright reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave-pulse signal.

For the stated setup, mod_depth = 1 gives about a 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so a 52 ns pulse is approximately a pi pulse. On resonance this should produce a substantial localized fluorescence drop in the post-pulse readout relative to the bright reference, with possible contrast on the order of the 22 percent m_S=0 to m_S=+1 scale.

The combined traces do not show a robust localized dip of readout 2 relative to readout 1. The largest separations are broad and inconsistent with the per-average overlays; the two stored averages differ strongly in baseline and slope, consistent with tracking cadence or drift rather than independent repeatability. Around the high-frequency side readout 2 is often comparable to or above readout 1, and the apparent low-frequency deficit is not narrow or repeatable enough for a pODMR resonance. I therefore classify this case as resonance absent.

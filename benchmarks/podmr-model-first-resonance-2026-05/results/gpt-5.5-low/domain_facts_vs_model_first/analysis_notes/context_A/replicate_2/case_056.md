The provided sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz. The active instructions first polarize and detect a true m_S = 0 reference, then because full_expt = 0 they skip the optional m_S = 1 reference block, apply one rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse, and detect again. Thus readout 1 is the polarized reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave signal readout.

The sequence parameters relevant to the pulse are length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1 in the provided XML/variable values. With the supplied setup facts, mod_depth = 1 corresponds to about 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so a 52 ns pulse is approximately a pi pulse on resonance and should produce a large contrast change, on the order of the 22% m_S = 0 to m_S = +1 contrast scale, if the swept microwave frequency crosses a strong single-NV resonance.

The data do not show that pattern. Both readouts share a broad downward drift over the scan and both have a local low point near 3.875 GHz, including the reference readout. The post-pulse readout is not consistently or sharply suppressed relative to the reference by anything close to the expected contrast; the apparent differences are only a few percent and change sign at several frequencies. The two stored averages also mainly show cadence/tracking-like offsets rather than a stable independent repeated resonance feature. I therefore classify this case as resonance absent.

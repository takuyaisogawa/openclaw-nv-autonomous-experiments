The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml, varying mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The instruction order acquires a true mS=0 optical reference first after polarization, then skips the mS=1 reference because full_expt = 0, then applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time and acquires the signal readout. Thus readout 1 is the mS=0 reference and readout 2 is the microwave-pulse signal, not two independent pODMR repeats.

The saved variable table gives mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With the stated setup Rabi frequency of about 10 MHz at mod_depth = 1, the pulse duration is approximately a pi pulse on resonance. A real single-NV pODMR resonance should therefore produce a strong localized reduction of the signal readout relative to the mS=0 reference, on the order of the setup contrast scale, about 22%, allowing for imperfect contrast.

The combined data do not show such a feature. The signal/reference contrast fluctuates around zero, with several isolated or repeated negative signal excursions of only about 6-7% at 3.885-3.890, 3.910, and 3.925 GHz, plus positive excursions elsewhere. The pattern is not a clean localized resonance, and the per-average overlays mainly show baseline/tracking offsets rather than reliable independent reproduction of a spectral dip. Given the strong expected pulse at mod_depth = 1 and 52 ns, the observed small scattered dips are insufficient evidence for a pODMR resonance.

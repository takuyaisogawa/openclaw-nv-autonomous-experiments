The provided sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The active instructions polarize, detect the bright mS=0 reference, wait, then apply a rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse and detect again. The full_expt variable is 0, so the optional mS=+1 reference block is inactive; readout 1 is the polarized mS=0 reference and readout 2 is the post-Rabi-pulse signal. The sequence XML variables give mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns.

Using the stated setup calibration, mod_depth = 1 gives about a 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so a 52 ns pulse is approximately a pi pulse on resonance. If a pODMR resonance were present, the post-pulse readout should show a substantial dip relative to the mS=0 reference, with a scale comparable to the roughly 22% mS=0 to mS=+1 contrast.

The combined readouts do not show such a feature. The signal-reference differences are only a few percent and change sign across the sweep, with local negative excursions near 3.850, 3.905, and 3.915 GHz but no coherent resonance-shaped response. The per-average overlays indicate these variations are not stable enough to treat the two stored averages as a strong independent repeatability test; they look consistent with tracking/noise-scale fluctuations rather than a robust ODMR contrast feature. Therefore I classify this case as resonance absent.

The provided sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz. With full_expt = 0, the active measurement does not acquire the optional m_S = +1 reference; the two stored readouts are the initial polarized bright/reference detection followed by the detection after a rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time microwave pulse. The active microwave pulse uses length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1 from the provided sequence values, corresponding to roughly a pi pulse for the stated 10 MHz Rabi frequency scale.

Given a pi-like pulse, an on-resonance transition should make the post-pulse readout lower than the bright reference, with an expected contrast scale up to about 22 percent. The combined data show readout 2 dropping strongly around 3.875-3.885 GHz, reaching about 40.5 counts while the reference readout is around 44.4-47.9 counts in that region. This is a localized, resonance-like depression of the post-pulse signal rather than a broad monotonic drift. The per-average overlay also shows a trough in the post-pulse readout around the same points, though the two stored averages should not be overinterpreted as an independent repeatability test because averages may reflect tracking cadence.

Decision: a pODMR resonance is present.

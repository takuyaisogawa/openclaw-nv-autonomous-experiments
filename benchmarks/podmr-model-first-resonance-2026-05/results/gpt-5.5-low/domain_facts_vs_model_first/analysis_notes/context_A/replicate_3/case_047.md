The provided sequence XML is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz. The active sequence first polarizes and detects the true m_S = 0 bright reference, skips the optional m_S = 1 reference because full_expt = 0, then applies one rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse and detects again. Thus readout 1 is the no-microwave bright reference and readout 2 is the signal after the microwave pulse.

The relevant pulse has length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1. With the stated setup scale of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, this is near a pi pulse on resonance, so a real pODMR resonance should produce a sizeable drop in the post-pulse readout relative to the bright reference, on the order of the setup contrast scale if the drive is resonant and effective.

The measured readout 2 does not show a clear resonance-like dip relative to readout 1. Instead, the two traces are close, noisy, and readout 2 is often equal to or above readout 1, including near the highest excursion around 3.915 GHz. The per-average overlays mainly show tracking-level offsets and do not provide a stable repeated resonance feature. Since the pulse should be strong enough to reveal a clear ODMR contrast if on resonance, the data do not support a pODMR resonance being present.

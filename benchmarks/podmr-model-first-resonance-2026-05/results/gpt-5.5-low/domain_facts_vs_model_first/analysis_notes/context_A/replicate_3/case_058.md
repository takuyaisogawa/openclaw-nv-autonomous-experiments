Sequence XML and raw export indicate an active Rabimodulated.xml pODMR frequency sweep over mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz with 21 points. The active instructions polarize and detect first, then skip the optional 1-level reference because full_expt is 0, then apply rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time followed by a second detection. Therefore readout 1 is the mS=0 bright reference/tracking readout, and readout 2 is the resonance-sensitive readout after the microwave pulse.

Using the provided sequence XML values, mod_depth is 1 and length_rabi_pulse is 52 ns. With the supplied setup fact of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth 1, this is approximately a pi-scale pulse, so an on-resonance response should strongly transfer population toward mS=+1 and produce a sizable readout-2 fluorescence reduction relative to the mS=0 reference, on the order of the stated 22% contrast scale.

The measured readouts do not show such a dip. Readout 2 is generally comparable to or higher than readout 1 across the sweep, with an upward drift toward high frequency rather than a localized resonance-like contrast loss. The per-average traces show cadence/drift offsets and scatter, not a consistent independent repeat of a resonance feature. Because the expected pi-scale contrast signature is absent, I decide that a pODMR resonance is absent in this case.

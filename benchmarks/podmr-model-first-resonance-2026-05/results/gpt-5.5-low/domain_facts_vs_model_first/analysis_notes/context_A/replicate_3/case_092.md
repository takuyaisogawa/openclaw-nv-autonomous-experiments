The provided sequence is Rabimodulated.xml. It first polarizes and detects a true mS=0 reference, waits, then because full_expt is 0 it skips the optional mS=+1 reference block. The active measurement then applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1, followed by detection. Thus readout 1 is the no-microwave/0-level reference and readout 2 is the post-Rabi-pulse signal.

With the stated setup, mod_depth = 1 implies roughly 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so a 52 ns pulse is close to a pi pulse on resonance. A real pODMR resonance should therefore produce a clear reduction of the post-pulse readout relative to the 0 reference, on the order of a substantial fraction of the about 22% contrast scale. The combined traces show readout 2 sometimes below and sometimes above readout 1, with only small point-to-point differences. The most visible depression around the high-frequency side is also accompanied by drift/tracking-like changes in the reference and is not a robust, isolated contrast feature. The two stored averages are not strong independent repeatability evidence and show considerable scatter.

Decision: resonance_absent. The active pulse is strong enough that an on-resonance response should be much larger and more coherent than the observed small noisy readout differences.

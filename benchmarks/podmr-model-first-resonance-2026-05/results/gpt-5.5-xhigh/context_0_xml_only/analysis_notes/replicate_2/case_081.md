I used the supplied sequence XML and raw readout export for this case only.

The active sequence is Rabimodulated while varying mw_freq from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The instructions first acquire a true 0-level reference after optical polarization, then skip the optional 1-level reference block because full_expt = 0, then apply the active rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse and acquire the signal readout. Thus readout 1 is the no-MW/0-level reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave signal readout. The active pulse duration is length_rabi_pulse = 5.2e-08 s, which is 52 ns after sample-rate rounding at 250 MHz. The active modulation depth value is mod_depth = 1.

The raw signal is noisy, but the signal-minus-reference contrast has its strongest negative feature in the middle of the sweep. The combined readout difference reaches its most negative value at about 3.885 GHz, and a three-point smoothing of the difference is also most negative around 3.885 GHz. The individual averages put their most negative signal-minus-reference points in the same central region, near 3.89 to 3.90 GHz. This central negative contrast is consistent with a pODMR resonance being present, despite point-to-point scatter and some common-mode reference fluctuations.

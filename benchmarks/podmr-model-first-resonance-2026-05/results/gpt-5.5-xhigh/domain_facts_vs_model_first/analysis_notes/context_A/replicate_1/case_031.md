The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq scanned from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The pulse sequence first polarizes the NV and immediately detects the true m_S = 0 bright reference. Because full_expt = 0, the optional m_S = 1 reference block is skipped. The second acquired readout is therefore the signal after a Rabi-modulated microwave pulse followed by detection.

From the provided sequence XML and exported variable values, mod_depth is 1 and length_rabi_pulse is 52 ns. With the stated setup scale of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, 52 ns is near a pi pulse. Therefore, if the scan crosses the NV transition, readout 2 should be suppressed relative to readout 1 by a sizable fraction of the approximately 22% available m_S = 0 to m_S = +1 contrast. Away from resonance the two readouts should be much closer, aside from drift and tracking effects.

The combined readouts show a localized and structured dip only in the post-pulse readout near 3.875 to 3.880 GHz. At 3.875 GHz readout 1 is about 47.83 while readout 2 is about 39.65, a drop of about 17.1%; at 3.880 GHz the drop is about 16.9%. Neighboring points form a trough rather than a single isolated outlier, and the first readout stays in its usual bright range. The two stored averages both contribute to the suppression, though the averages should not be over-interpreted as independent repeatability because they may reflect tracking cadence.

This is the expected signature for a pODMR resonance under this near-pi-pulse sequence, so I classify the case as resonance_present.

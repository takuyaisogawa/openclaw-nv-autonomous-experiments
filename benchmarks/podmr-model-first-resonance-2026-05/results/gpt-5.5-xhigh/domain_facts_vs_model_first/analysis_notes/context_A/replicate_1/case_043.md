The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The instructions first acquire a true m_S = 0 reference after optical polarization, then skip the optional 1-level reference because full_expt = 0, then apply rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time and acquire the driven readout. Thus readout 1 is the 0-reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave driven readout.

The relevant pulse is length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns with mod_depth = 1. At the stated setup scale, mod_depth = 1 gives about a 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so 52 ns is essentially a pi pulse. If the microwave frequency were resonant, readout 2 should be substantially lower than readout 1, on the order of the known 22% m_S = 0 to m_S = +1 contrast scale.

The data do not show that. The largest negative readout2 - readout1 excursion is only about -5.6%, and the sign changes across the sweep with positive excursions of similar practical size. The two stored averages should not be treated as a strong repeatability test, and the overlay is consistent with tracking/noise-dominated variation rather than a clear pODMR resonance. I therefore decide that no reliable resonance is present.

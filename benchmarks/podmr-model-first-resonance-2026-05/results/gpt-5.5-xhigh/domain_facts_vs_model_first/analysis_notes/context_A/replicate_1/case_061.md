The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq scanned from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The executable path first polarizes and detects the bright m_S = 0 reference, waits, skips the optional +1 reference because full_expt = 0, then applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1 before the second detection. Thus readout 1 is the 0-level reference and readout 2 is the post-Rabi-pulse signal.

At mod_depth = 1 the stated Rabi frequency is about 10 MHz, so a 52 ns pulse is approximately a pi pulse on resonance. With the setup contrast scale of about 22%, an on-resonance response should make the post-pulse signal readout substantially lower than the 0 reference, on the order of a large fraction of that contrast. The observed combined readouts do not show that behavior: readout 2 averages only 0.32 counts below readout 1, the mean ratio is about 0.994, and the differences fluctuate in sign across the scan. The largest negative points are only a few counts and are not a clean resonance feature; stored averages mainly reflect tracking cadence and do not supply a strong independent repeatability check.

Decision: resonance_absent.

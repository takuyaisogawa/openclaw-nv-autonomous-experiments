With the stated setup scale of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, a 52 ns pulse is approximately a pi pulse, so a real resonance should produce a large fluorescence drop in the post-pulse readout, up to the order of the stated 22 percent m_S = 0 to m_S = +1 contrast.

The raw data match that expectation. Readout 1 stays near 41-43 counts across the central scan, while readout 2 shows a localized dip centered at 3.875-3.880 GHz: at 3.880 GHz, readout 1 is about 41.40 and readout 2 is about 33.10, a same-point drop of about 20 percent. The dip is also broad over adjacent frequency points, not a single isolated point. The two stored averages should not be treated as a strong independent repeatability test because they can reflect tracking cadence, but both show the same central post-pulse suppression. This is consistent with a pODMR resonance being present.

Active sequence: Rabimodulated.xml scanning mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The XML sets full_expt=0, so the branch that would collect a +1 reference is inactive. The first detection follows optical polarization and is the bright m_S=0 reference; the second detection follows rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time and is the signal after the microwave pulse. The active pulse uses length_rabi_pulse=52 ns, rounded unchanged at 250 MHz sample rate, with mod_depth=1.

With the stated 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth=1, 52 ns is approximately a pi pulse, so on resonance the post-pulse signal should approach the darker +1 level. The combined data show a localized dip of the post-pulse signal near 3.875-3.880 GHz: from about 49.27 to 39.77 at 3.875 GHz (19.3% contrast) and from about 47.04 to 38.96 at 3.880 GHz (17.2% contrast), close to the expected ~22% contrast scale. The neighboring frequencies recover, and both stored averages show their largest contrast in the same frequency region, though I treat the averages mainly as tracking-cadence snapshots rather than independent repeatability. This is consistent with a real pODMR resonance.

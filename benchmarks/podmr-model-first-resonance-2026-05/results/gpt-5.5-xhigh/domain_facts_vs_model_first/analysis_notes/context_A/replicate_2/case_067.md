The provided sequence XML is Rabimodulated.xml. In the active instructions, the experiment first polarizes and detects the pumped mS = 0 level reference. Because full_expt = 0, the optional mS = +1 reference block is skipped. The sequence then applies one rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse and performs the second detection, so readout 1 is the mS = 0 reference and readout 2 is the post-microwave signal readout.

The active microwave pulse uses length_rabi_pulse = 5.2e-08 s, rounded at 250 MS/s to 52 ns, with mod_depth = 1. With the stated setup scale, mod_depth = 1 gives about a 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so 52 ns is close to a pi pulse. If a pODMR resonance were present, readout 2 should show a smooth, frequency-localized reduction relative to readout 1 approaching the 22% mS = 0 to mS = +1 contrast scale.

The combined readouts do not show that behavior. The largest negative differential points are only about -7.2% at 3.880 GHz and -7.0% at 3.890 GHz, while the same scan has a positive excursion of about +8.4% at 3.830 GHz and the apparent negative points are not a smooth contiguous resonance feature. The per-average traces are dominated by tracking-scale drift, so they do not provide a strong independent repeatability test. Overall the active near-pi pulse and expected contrast scale make the observed weak, irregular differential features insufficient evidence for a pODMR resonance.

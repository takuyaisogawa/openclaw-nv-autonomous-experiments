The provided sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq scanned from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The active instructions first acquire a true m_S = 0 reference after optical polarization, then, because full_expt = 0, skip the separate m_S = 1 reference block and acquire the pulsed measurement after a rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time pulse.

The active measurement pulse has length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1. With the given setup scale of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, this is approximately a pi pulse. If a pODMR resonance were present, the second readout should be driven toward m_S = +1 and be much darker than the first readout near resonance, on the order of the stated 22% contrast scale.

The combined data do not show that behavior. The largest same-point suppression of readout 2 relative to readout 1 is about 3.06 counts at 3.890 GHz, a ratio of about 0.936, while the average ratio is about 0.989 and the readouts cross several times. The per-average traces are dominated by tracking-level offsets between stored averages, and those averages are not a strong independent repeatability test here. The small, irregular readout separation is far below the expected near-pi contrast signature, so I decide that no pODMR resonance is present.

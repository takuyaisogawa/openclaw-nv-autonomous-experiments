The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml. With full_expt = 0, the conditional 1-level reference block is inactive; the acquired roles are a polarized bright m_S = 0 reference readout followed by the signal readout after the microwave Rabi pulse. The active pulse is rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1.

Using the stated setup scale, mod_depth = 1 gives about a 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so a 52 ns pulse is essentially a pi pulse. On resonance this should transfer a large fraction of population out of m_S = 0 and produce a strong fluorescence dip in the post-pulse signal readout, with an upper contrast scale of about 22% relative to the bright reference.

The data show that behavior. The reference readout stays broadly near 41 to 43 counts across the central region, while the post-pulse signal readout drops from an off-resonant level near 42 counts to about 34.7 to 35.7 counts around 3.875 to 3.880 GHz. At the deepest point the signal is roughly 17% below the simultaneous reference, which is a substantial fraction of the expected contrast scale and is much larger than a small tracking fluctuation. The two stored averages should not be over-weighted as independent repeatability, but both show a pronounced central signal depression near the same frequency.

Decision: a pODMR resonance is present.

The provided sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz. The active instructions first polarize and detect a true m_s = 0 reference, then skip the optional 1-level reference because full_expt = 0, then apply rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time using length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1 before the second detection. With the stated setup, mod_depth = 1 gives an approximately 10 MHz Rabi frequency, so 52 ns is near a pi pulse; if the swept microwave frequency hits the NV transition, readout 2 should show a clear fluorescence dip relative to readout 1, potentially on the order of the 22% contrast scale and with a width spanning at least nearby 5 MHz scan points.

The combined readouts do not show that pattern. The readout-2-minus-readout-1 difference has mean about 0.07 counts and standard deviation about 1.48 counts. The largest negative excursion is at 3.890 GHz, about -3.27 counts or -6.9%, but it is a single-point dip bracketed by positive or near-zero differences; the three-point windows remain small, with the most negative three-point mean around -1.10 counts. Other negative excursions appear at unrelated frequencies, such as 3.830 and 3.850 GHz, which argues against a coherent pODMR line. The per-average traces also contain substantial tracking-like drift, and the stored averages should not be treated as strong independent repeatability evidence.

Decision: resonance_absent. The observed fluctuations are too small, isolated, and irregular for a reliable pODMR resonance under this near-pi-pulse sequence.

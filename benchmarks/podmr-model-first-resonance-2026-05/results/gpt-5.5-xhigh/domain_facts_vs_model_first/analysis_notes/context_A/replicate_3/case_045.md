The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. In the instructions, the first detection occurs after polarization and is the true m_S = 0 reference. Because full_expt = 0, the optional +1 reference block is skipped. The second active detection follows rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1, so readout 2 is the post-Rabi-pulse signal.

At mod_depth = 1 the supplied setup fact gives an approximate 10 MHz Rabi frequency, making the 52 ns pulse essentially a pi pulse. If the scanned microwave frequency hit a pODMR resonance, the post-pulse readout should drop strongly relative to the 0-level reference, on the order of the stated 22% contrast scale.

The measured readout 2 trace stays close to readout 1. The combined normalized differences fluctuate around zero, with the largest apparent dip about 7% at one point near 3.920 GHz and smaller isolated dips elsewhere. The neighboring scan points do not form a coherent resonance feature, and the per-average overlays show sizable tracking/count offsets rather than a stable independent resonance line. Since the expected near-pi-pulse response would be much larger and more structured, I classify this case as resonance absent.

Sequence review: the active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq scanned from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The instructions first polarize and detect, giving readout 1 as the mS = 0 reference. Since full_expt = 0, the explicit mS = +1 reference block is skipped. The sequence then applies rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns, mod_depth = 1, and switch_delay = 100 ns, followed by detection, so readout 2 is the signal after the microwave pulse.

At mod_depth = 1 the stated Rabi frequency is about 10 MHz, so a 52 ns pulse is close to a pi pulse. A resonance should therefore appear as readout 2 dropping below the readout 1 reference at the resonant frequency. The strongest normalized suppression is at scan value 3.860 GHz, where readout 1 is 53.44 and readout 2 is 48.85, about an 8.6% drop. Both stored averages have the same sign at this point, though the stored averages should not be treated as a strong independent repeatability test. The observed dip is smaller than the full 22% mS = 0 to mS = +1 contrast scale and the scan is noisy, but the localized readout-2 suppression has the expected polarity for the active near-pi pulse sequence.

Decision: resonance present.

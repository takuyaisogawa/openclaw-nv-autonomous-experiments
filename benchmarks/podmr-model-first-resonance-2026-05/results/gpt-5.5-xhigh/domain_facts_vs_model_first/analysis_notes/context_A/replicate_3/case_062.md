The provided sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq swept from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz. The active instructions first polarize and detect a true m_S=0 reference, then wait, then apply rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse and mod_depth before the second detection. full_expt is 0, so the optional m_S=+1 reference block is disabled. Thus readout 1 is the pre-microwave m_S=0 reference and readout 2 is the post-pulse signal.

The relevant pulse settings are mod_depth = 1 and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With the stated calibration of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, this is essentially a pi-scale pulse. If the microwave frequency crossed a real pODMR resonance, the post-pulse signal should show a clear dark-state dip relative to the reference, with a possible scale approaching the 22% m_S=0 to m_S=+1 contrast.

The averaged readout2/readout1 ratios instead show only scattered small excursions. The deepest averaged drop is near 3.850 GHz at about 0.927, roughly a 7% effect, and other negative differences appear at isolated points without a coherent resonance line shape. The per-average traces also move substantially with tracking-like baseline variation, so the apparent dips are not strong independent evidence. Given the pi-scale pulse and expected contrast, the data do not support calling a pODMR resonance present.

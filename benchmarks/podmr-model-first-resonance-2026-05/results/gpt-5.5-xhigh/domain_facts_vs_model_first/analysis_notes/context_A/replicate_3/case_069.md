The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml, sweeping mw_freq from 3.825 to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. In the provided sequence XML, full_expt is 0, so the optional m_S = +1 reference branch is disabled despite do_adiabatic_inversion being true. The active readouts are therefore: readout 1 is the true m_S = 0 reference immediately after optical polarization, and readout 2 is the signal readout after the Rabi-modulated microwave pulse.

The microwave pulse is rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time with length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns and mod_depth = 1. With the stated setup scale of about 10 MHz Rabi frequency at mod_depth = 1, the pulse is close to a pi pulse. If a resonance were present, the post-pulse signal readout should drop strongly relative to the m_S = 0 reference, on the order of the 22% contrast scale for near-complete transfer.

The combined readout 2 / readout 1 ratios stay near unity. The lowest combined ratios are about 0.94 at a few scan points, corresponding to only 5-6% dips, and there are also positive excursions of similar size. The stored averages do not show a stable frequency-localized dip: their strongest negative ratios occur at different scan points, and the per-average traces show broad tracking-like level shifts rather than a repeatable resonance feature. Given the near-pi pulse condition, the observed changes are too small and inconsistent to support a pODMR resonance.

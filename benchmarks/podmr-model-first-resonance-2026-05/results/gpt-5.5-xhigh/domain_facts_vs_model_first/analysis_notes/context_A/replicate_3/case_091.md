Sequence XML identifies the active sequence as Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq scanned from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps. The active variables are full_expt = 0, mod_depth = 1, and length_rabi_pulse = 52 ns. With full_expt = 0, the optional mS = +1 reference block is skipped, so the two active detections are: readout 1 after polarization as the bright mS = 0 reference, and readout 2 after the 52 ns modulated microwave pulse as the pODMR measurement.

For this setup, mod_depth = 1 implies a Rabi frequency of about 10 MHz. A 52 ns pulse is therefore close to a pi pulse, so an on-resonance transition should transfer a large fraction of population from mS = 0 to mS = +1 and produce a clear drop in readout 2 relative to readout 1. Given the stated setup contrast scale of about 22%, a real resonance would be expected to show a much larger and more coherent fluorescence reduction than the few-percent fluctuations seen here.

The combined data do not show a consistent dip of readout 2 against the bright reference. The largest positive separation is about 2.19 counts, or about 4.2%, at the lower scan edge; other candidate separations are similarly small and interleaved with opposite-sign points where readout 2 exceeds readout 1. The per-average traces are dominated by tracking-like baseline shifts, and the small point-to-point structure is not a strong independent repeatability test. Overall the readout differences are too small, inconsistent in sign, and not shaped like a convincing pODMR resonance for this near-pi-pulse sequence.

Decision: resonance_absent.

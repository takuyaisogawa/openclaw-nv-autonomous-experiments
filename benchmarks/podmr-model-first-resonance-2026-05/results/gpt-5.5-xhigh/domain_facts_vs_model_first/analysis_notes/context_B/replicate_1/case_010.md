Case: podmr_017_2026-05-12-134151

Inputs considered: inputs/sequence.xml, inputs/raw_export.json, and the raw readout plot.

Active sequence and readout roles:
- The active sequence is Rabimodulated.xml with mw_freq scanned from 3.825 GHz to 3.925 GHz in 5 MHz steps.
- The XML instructions first polarize the NV and call detection before any microwave pulse. This is the true m_s = 0 bright reference, so it corresponds to readout 1.
- full_expt = 0, so the optional m_s = 1 reference block is skipped.
- The active pODMR signal is then generated by rabi_pulse_mod_wait_time(PSeq, sample_rate, length_rabi_pulse, mod_depth, switch_delay, ch_on), followed by detection. This corresponds to readout 2.
- mod_depth = 1. The pulse length is 52 ns; with sample_rate = 250 MHz, round(52 ns * 250 MHz) = 13 samples, so the rounded duration remains 52 ns.

Quantitative physical expectation:
- Given the setup Rabi frequency of about 10 MHz at mod_depth = 1, the driven two-level model is
  P1(Delta) = f_R^2 / (f_R^2 + Delta^2) * sin^2(pi * t * sqrt(f_R^2 + Delta^2)),
  with f_R = 10 MHz and t = 52 ns.
- On resonance, P1(0) = sin^2(pi * 10e6 * 52e-9) = 0.996.
- With a 22% m_s = 0 to m_s = +1 contrast scale, the expected on-resonance raw PL drop of the post-pulse readout relative to the bright reference is 0.22 * 0.996 = 21.9%.
- At detunings of 2.5, 5, and 10 MHz, the same model gives expected drops of about 20.4%, 16.5%, and 6.0%, respectively. Thus a resonance within the scanned range should create a large one- to two-point dip in readout 2 relative to readout 1.

Observed quantitative comparison:
- The normalized contrast y = (readout1 - readout2) / readout1 ranges from -10.3% to +8.7%, with mean -0.45%.
- The largest observed positive drop is 8.7% at 3.855 GHz, far below the expected 21.9% on-resonance drop and not accompanied by the expected neighboring-point structure.
- For a typical reference level near 23 counts, the model predicts an on-resonance post-pulse value near 23 * (1 - 0.219) = 18.0 counts. The combined post-pulse readout never approaches that scale; its minimum is about 21.65 counts.
- I also scanned the above Rabi model over possible center frequencies and fit the normalized contrast with a linear baseline plus a resonance term. The best free-amplitude fit gave only an 8.9% resonance amplitude near 3.839 GHz, much smaller than the expected 22%. Forcing the expected 22% amplitude worsened the residual relative to a baseline-only fit.
- The two stored averages show strong opposite tracking trends, so I do not treat them as a strong independent repeatability test; they also do not reveal a consistent expected-scale resonance after considering the readout roles.

Decision:
The expected pODMR signal for the active 52 ns, mod_depth = 1 pulse should be a large approximately 22% dip in readout 2 relative to the m_s = 0 reference. The observed normalized readouts show only small, inconsistent fluctuations and no expected-scale Rabi resonance feature. Therefore the pODMR resonance is absent.
